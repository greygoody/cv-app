## Candidate statement

Morgan describes the installation responsibility as “basically the technical lead” because others depended on Morgan during technical failures.

## Observed facts

- Morgan authored the integration diagram and recovery guide.
- Morgan connected the lighting, sensor, and projection-control surfaces.
- The production manager asked Morgan to coordinate troubleshooting during installation week.
- Morgan trained two technicians in common recovery procedures.
- Formal staffing, budget, creative direction, and project approval remained elsewhere.
- No quantitative reliability or recovery improvement was measured.

## Interpretations considered

### Whole-exhibition technical lead

Rejected as too broad. The evidence does not support project-wide authority, budget, creative direction, staffing, or final approval.

### Installation-week integration owner and operational handoff lead

Supported as the strongest durable contribution model. It preserves coordination, technical responsibility, documentation, and training without implying organization-wide management.

### Documentation-focused technician

Rejected as too weak. It omits integration ownership and troubleshooting coordination supported by the diagram, manager email, and training note.

## Proposed framing

> Owned technical integration and troubleshooting coordination during installation week, connecting lighting, sensor, and projection-control systems, documenting recovery procedures, and training two technicians for operational handoff.

Lifecycle status: `limited` until the exact duration and regular-operation scope are confirmed.

Visibility: `cv-safe` for the synthetic fixture.

## Retained unknowns

- Whether Morgan continued as the primary integration owner after installation week.
- Number and type of failures handled.
- Recovery-time, reliability, uptime, visitor, or cost effects.

## Prohibited inferences

- Formal project-wide technical-lead title.
- People management, hiring, budget, or creative authority.
- Quantified reliability or commercial impact.

## Closure

The session should not force a binary choice between “technical lead” and “only technician.” It closes with a narrower leadership model that is evidence-backed and legible, while retaining the formal-title and quantified-effect questions.
