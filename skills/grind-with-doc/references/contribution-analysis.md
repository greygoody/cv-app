# Contribution analysis

Use this reference when the active focus concerns what the candidate actually did, owned, changed, or can safely claim.

## OMEP core

For each material contribution, distinguish:

1. **Context**: product, system, team, problem, and starting state.
2. **Ownership**: assigned responsibility, actual authority, collaborators, and explicit non-ownership.
3. **Mechanism**: technical decision, implementation, architecture, process, or intervention.
4. **Effect**: what became possible, safer, faster, clearer, more reliable, or less manual.
5. **Proof**: code, prototype, test, document, demonstration, operation, adoption, artifact, or explicit candidate correction.
6. **Quantification**: measured business effect, engineering effect, scope, proof count, or recoverable measurement.
7. **Boundary**: incomplete work, rejected inference, deferred scope, or responsibility limit.

The compact form is Ownership, Mechanism, Effect, Proof. Add context, quantification, and boundary whenever they change interpretation.

## Investigation prompts

Ask only questions that could materially change the active model:

- Which decisions were actually yours?
- Who depended on those decisions?
- What did collaborators own?
- What changed because of your work?
- What surviving artifact or witness supports it?
- Was the result adopted, demonstrated, operated, or only proposed?
- Which measurement is known, approximate, recoverable, or unavailable?
- Which stronger interpretation must remain prohibited?

## Safe composition

A contribution record may support:

- one canonical, target-independent statement;
- safe short variants that preserve the same meaning;
- target tags and selection guidance;
- evidence questions;
- prohibited inferences.

A projection may select and compress supported contribution units. It may not convert:

- design into implementation;
- prototype into production;
- participation into ownership;
- qualitative consequence into invented metrics;
- a team contribution into sole ownership;
- private proof into public inspectability.

## Quantification hierarchy

Prefer, in order:

1. observed business or user effect;
2. observed engineering effect;
3. observed scope measurement;
4. observed proof measurement;
5. concrete qualitative consequence with explicit proof;
6. mechanism and boundary when no effect was measured.

A missing number is an evidence question, not a blank cheque for percentages.
