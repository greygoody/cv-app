# Closure and promotion

A grind session is successful when one bounded focus reaches an explicit state. It does not need to produce a complete CV or even final wording.

## Closure states

- `resolved-promoted`: confirmed material was promoted through the active workspace contract;
- `resolved-proposed`: a reviewable proposal exists but is not yet canonical;
- `resolved-rejected`: one or more misleading interpretations were closed;
- `deferred-evidence-needed`: the next decision depends on named evidence;
- `blocked-candidate-decision`: evidence is sufficient but the candidate has not chosen among valid alternatives;
- `unresolved-but-bounded`: uncertainty remains, but its scope and consequences are now explicit.

## Closure contents

Record:

1. candidate identity or workspace boundary;
2. active focus;
3. source material consulted;
4. candidate statements retained;
5. observations accepted;
6. interpretations considered;
7. proposals made;
8. candidate confirmations;
9. rejected interpretations;
10. retained unknowns;
11. evidence worth recovering;
12. downstream records affected;
13. next useful focus, when known.

## Promotion proposal

A proposal must remain reviewable independently from the conversation that produced it.

It should contain:

- proposal ID and version;
- candidate ID;
- source and session references;
- proposed canonical change;
- epistemic and lifecycle status;
- visibility;
- evidence and proof references;
- limits and prohibited inferences;
- candidate confirmation state;
- expected current revision when mutation will occur;
- downstream impact;
- unresolved questions.

## Confirmation

Confirmation is scoped. A candidate may confirm a contribution for one project period while rejecting it as a permanent identity. A candidate may confirm truth while marking it private or target-specific.

Silence, continued conversation, or acceptance of polished wording does not automatically confirm the underlying claim.

## Historical integrity

Do not rewrite prior applications after promotion. New evidence may alter future projections and create review tasks, but history remains evidence of what was actually sent.
