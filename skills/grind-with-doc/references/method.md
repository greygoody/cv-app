# Evidence and promotion method

This method is candidate-agnostic. It defines how uncertain material may become durable professional truth.

## Authority progression

```text
source or candidate statement
  -> observations
  -> interpretations and hypotheses
  -> candidate-reviewed proposal
  -> confirmed canonical record
  -> downstream impact review
```

A source is provenance, not truth merely because it exists. A polished statement is not stronger evidence than a rough one.

## Epistemic status

- `observed`: directly supported by inspected material or an explicit candidate correction;
- `deduction`: conclusion derived from named observations;
- `proposal`: interpretation, framing, selection, wording, or plan awaiting acceptance;
- `unknown`: materially unresolved.

## Lifecycle status

- `accepted`: supported and safe within the recorded scope;
- `limited`: supported only with narrower wording or maturity boundaries;
- `planned`: intended work, never present as completed;
- `rejected`: unsupported, misleading, or candidate-rejected;
- `superseded`: replaced by a newer accepted record;
- `stale`: once useful, but material accuracy requires review;
- `unknown`: no adequate conclusion exists.

Epistemic status and lifecycle status answer different questions. `observed` describes how something is known. `accepted` describes whether it currently governs use.

## Visibility

- `public`: safe for unrestricted public use;
- `cv-safe`: safe in an ordinary CV or application;
- `private-summary-only`: may be summarized without exposing underlying detail;
- `confidential`: available only within the private candidate workspace;
- `never-export`: must not appear in external artifacts.

A derived output may not broaden source visibility without explicit review.

## Promotion rules

A promotion proposal must:

1. bind one candidate;
2. reference the source or statement being interpreted;
3. state the proposed fact, contribution, identity model, or framing;
4. preserve observed, deduced, proposed, and unknown material separately;
5. state claim limits and prohibited inferences;
6. state visibility;
7. record candidate confirmation status;
8. identify affected downstream records;
9. retain material unresolved questions;
10. never silently rewrite historical applications.

## Change and invalidation

Material upstream changes should invalidate only dependent decisions or reviews. They should not silently synchronize copied wording across the workspace.

Historical applications remain immutable. New evidence creates a new projection or future application decision.

## Target projection boundary

A target-specific CV may select, order, compress, and use understandable target vocabulary. It may not change ownership, mechanism, effect, proof, measurement state, maturity, or visibility.
